Why it's slow:

Step 2: Prepare the SSD

  1. Plug your SSD into your PC or the Pi via USB 3.0.

  2. Use Raspberry Pi Imager (on your PC or Pi) to flash Raspberry Pi OS directly to the SSD.

Step 3: Boot from SSD

  1. Power off the Pi.

  2. Unplug the microSD card.

  3. Connect the SSD to a blue USB 3.0 port on the Pi.

  4. Power up the Pi – it should boot from the SSD directly.

Step 4: Verify

Once booted, run:

lsblk

You should see your SSD as /dev/sda1 mounted as /boot and / (root).

You can enable USB quirks if you experience boot issues or slow performance:

  1. Edit cmdline.txt: 

  1. sudo nano /boot/cmdline.txt

Good question! Since you already have a working microSD setup with installed software, you’ll want to clone your current microSD card to the SSD, so everything (OS, apps, configs, etc.) is exactly as it is now.

Here’s how to clone your microSD to SSD:

Step 1: Plug in both drives

  • Your Pi should boot from the microSD card as usual.

  • Plug the SSD into a USB 3.0 port.

Step 2: Identify drives

Run:

lsblk

  • Your microSD will likely be /dev/mmcblk0 (with partitions /dev/mmcblk0p1, /dev/mmcblk0p2, etc.).

  • Your SSD will show as /dev/sda (e.g., /dev/sda1, /dev/sda2, etc.).

Step 3: Clone with rsync

1. First, format the SSD using gparted or fdisk if it's blank, then create two partitions:

  • boot partition (FAT32, about 256MB or match microSD’s /boot)

  • root partition (ext4, uses the rest of the space)

2. Mount both SSD partitions:

sudo mkdir /mnt/ssdboot /mnt/ssdroot
sudo mount /dev/sda1 /mnt/ssdboot
sudo mount /dev/sda2 /mnt/ssdroot

3. Mount your running system's boot:

sudo mount /boot /mnt/boot

4. Clone /boot:

sudo rsync -avx /mnt/boot/ /mnt/ssdboot/

5. Clone root (/):

sudo rsync -axv --exclude={"/dev/*","/proc/*","/sys/*","/tmp/*","/run/*","/mnt/*","/media/*","/lost+found"} / /mnt/ssdroot/
This keeps permissions and links intact.

Step 4: Adjust the SSD’s /etc/fstab

  1. Edit /mnt/ssdroot/etc/fstab: 

  1. sudo nano /mnt/ssdroot/etc/fstab

  2. Replace /dev/mmcblk0p1 and /dev/mmcblk0p2 with /dev/sda1 and /dev/sda2, respectively.

Step 5: Fix cmdline.txt

Edit /mnt/ssdboot/cmdline.txt:

sudo nano /mnt/ssdboot/cmdline.txt

Change:

root=/dev/mmcblk0p2

to:

root=/dev/sda2

Step 6: Reboot

  1. Shutdown the Pi: 

  1. sudo shutdown now

  2. Unplug the microSD card.

  3. Power up with just the SSD plugged in!

Done!

This method will preserve all your installed packages, configs, services, and tweaks. It’s like a 1:1 migration.
